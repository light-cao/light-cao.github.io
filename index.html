Using Language Model for Implementation of Emotional Text-To-Speech

Abstract:

With the development of neural network, text-to-speech (TTS) technology is booming unprecedentedly. The speech generated by modern text-to-speech systems almost sound as natural as human audio. However, the style control of synthetic speech usually limits to discrete emotion type and the emotion embedding which controls emotion transfer contains redundant transcript information. In this paper, we apply pre-trained language model BERT to our TTS system to achieve style control and transfer. Using BERT makes our proposed model study the relationship between text representations and acoustic emotion embedding. The experimental results show that our proposed model outperforms baseline GST-Tacotron2 model in both parallel and non-parallel style transfer.
